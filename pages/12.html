Caesar with shrimp

It is a rare case when a popular salad has an exact author. Meet Caesar Cardini, owner of an American bar in Tijuana, on the border with Mexico. It is believed that Caesar invented in 1920 a strange mixture of leaves, garlic and raw egg specifically to neutralize the effects of counterfeit whiskey, which even during prohibition in his bar flowed freely. Later, the salad grew to the restaurant level by adding chicken or shrimp to the recipe, and anchovies to the dressing.
